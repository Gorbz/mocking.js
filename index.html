Introduction
What is the goal of life?
Compute correlation functions in gauge theories. Especially in the IR.

$$ \left\langle \prod_i \mathcal{O}_i \right\rangle $$
In general this is very hard.

Use supersymmetry and other simplifying principles.
Seiberg and Witten were able make remarkable progress into the solution of $\mathcal{N}=2$ SYM in 1994.

Let me remind you.

  • We work with a $\mathcal{N}=2$ SYM theory with gauge group $SU(2)$,

  • The field content is a vector multiplet $(A, \phi, \psi, \lambda),$

  • The Coulomb branch is given by the minima of the potential $u = \langle {\rm Tr}(\phi^2)\rangle $

  • Coupling constant of the theory is $\tau = \frac{\theta}{2\pi}+ \frac{4\pi}{g^2}$

The Coulomb branch $\mathcal{B}$ is isomorphic to

$\mathcal{B} \cong \mathbb{CP}^1 / \{ \pm 1, \infty\}$

Due to Riemann's uniformization theorem though we have

$ \mathcal{B} \cong \mathcal{H}/\Gamma^0(4)$.
The Coulomb branch $\mathcal{B}$ looks as follows.


$u(\tau)$, the Coulomb branch parameter, is invariant under a $\Gamma^0(4)$ transformation.
Can we solve the theory?

Yes, if we consider a topological version of it.
